## Product: MyWally — Wallet Companion for Confident Financial Participation

A confidence layer embedded within an existing eWallet (TouchNGo). The goal is not to simplify features alone — it is to enable elderly users to participate in digital finance safely, with real-time support from trusted family at critical moments.

**Why:** Malaysia's elderly have access to digital wallets but are not confidently using them. The barrier is fear — fear of mistakes, fear of being scammed, fear of making wrong decisions with no one to consult.

**Core tagline:** "We are not building a financial tool — we are building a confidence layer on top of finance."

---

## Target Persona: Makcik Aisyah (60–75)

- Basic smartphone user (WhatsApp, simple apps)
- Limited or irregular income, relies partly on children
- Risk-averse, avoids making mistakes
- Problems:
  - Doesn't know if she has enough money daily
  - Finds financial apps overwhelming
  - Afraid of making wrong decisions
  - **No one to consult at the moment of transaction** ← new core problem
  - Avoids digital financial tools altogether

## Design Principles

1. Clarity over complexity — show only what matters now
2. Guidance over exploration — users ask, not navigate
3. Confidence over control — reduce fear before enabling action
4. Support over isolation — decisions are not made alone
5. Calm over alarm — avoid fear-based messaging

---

## MVP Modules (5)

1. **Elderly Mode (Enable)** — simplified UI inside existing eWallet, large buttons, minimal actions, clear balance and daily summary
2. **Wally (Engage)** — voice/button-based Q&A, simple answers ("Do I have enough?", "How much did I spend?"), human-readable insights
3. **Daily Budget & Feedback** — simple budget setup, daily spending feedback, status indicators (Safe / Near / Exceeded)
4. **Real-Time Support Layer (Empower)** ⭐ KEY DIFFERENTIATOR — detects unusual transaction behaviour, triggers AI call to caregiver, caregiver can approve or block, elderly user receives calm explanation and clear next step
5. **Trust & Guidance Layer** — gentle financial tips, reassurance messaging, no blame/no fear tone

---

## MVP Priority (Hackathon build scope)

**Must Have:** Elderly Mode UI, Wally (basic Q&A), Transaction flow + caregiver approval simulation, Blocked/approved UX states
**Nice to Have:** Voice interaction, Budget tracking, Safety tips

**How to apply:** When planning features or UI, always bias toward simplicity, large tap targets, and reducing cognitive load. The caregiver approval flow (Module 4) is the key differentiator — prioritise its UX being calm and clear. Features not in MVP scope should be flagged as post-MVP.
